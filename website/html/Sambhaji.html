Chhatrapati Sambhaji Maharaj was the eldest son of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj - the good Maratha warrior king. Shivaji Maharaj belonged to the Bhonsle clan and was born on 19th February 1627. Shivaji Maharaj built the Hindawi Swaraj - the Self-rule of Indians as against that of the Mughals who identified themselves as descendants from Tamerlane of Mongolia and also of Turcik - Central Asian (Chagtai) bloodline. .... Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj died on 3rd April 1680 but in his lifetime, he laid the inspiration for the genesis of the Maratha empire - a solid bulwark against invaders. His son Sambhaji Maharaj had an enormous task cut out before him - to defend the Maratha empire against the might of the Mughals in Delhi who were later subdued and decimated by the Maratha armies) and to hold on the vision and policies of Shivaji Maharaj.

ATTACK FROM MUGHALS:

In 1681, Aurangzeb's fourth son Akbar reached Deccan (variation for Dakshin or the South) and tried to consolidate forces against his father's rule out Delhi. To quell this rebellion and to undertake to end off the Maratha empire, Aurangzeb left Delhi with 4-5 lakh soldiers and headed to Khirki - what's today's Aurangabad in Maharashtra. In 1682, Aurangzeb tried attacking the Maratha Empire from all directions. He intended to use the Mughal numerical superiority to his advantage. Sambhaji had prepared well for the invasions and therefore the Maratha forces promptly engaged the numerically strong Mughal army in several small battles using guerilla warfare tactics.

GLORY:

British origin historian Dennis Kincaid writes, "He (Sambhaji) was ordered by the Emperor to embrace Islam. He refused and was made to run the gauntlet of the entire Imperial army. Tattered and bleeding he was brought before the Emperor and repeated his refusal. His tongue was torn and again the question was put. He involved writing material and wrote 'No, never!' So then he was put to death by torture". He was beheaded and his head was mounted on a pike that was then showed the general public so on demoralise them. "His sacrifice has earned Chhatrapati Sambhaji Maharaj the spot of reverence in the hearts of all. .... Aurangzeb managed to capture him by deceit on 1 February 1689. Sambhaji Maharaj was tortured for days and killed within the most heinous fashion and died on 11 March 1689. He showed exemplary courage in facing death only to guard three of the items he held most dear in life... Dev, Desh, and Dharma (God, Country, and Religion). that's the rationale why he's revered in Maharashtra. People realise and appreciate the very fact that he didn't fall prey to Aurangzeb's tactics, and chose to embrace death instead of betray the cause he stood for," an author of several books chronicling the Maratha empire's era said.
